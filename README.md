# IA520 Integrated Process Project

This vendor-neutral project develops one organizational scenario through four cumulative Classroom 50 milestones. It examines Systems, Applications and Products at the process, data, control, interface, and decision-design level. It does not require proprietary enterprise software or certification study.

## Project standard

Connect a customer outcome to internal roles, process states, data, decisions, controls, interfaces, tests, and operating evidence. Preserve what changes between milestones instead of silently replacing prior conclusions. Canvas is the official submission, rubric, feedback, and grade record.

## Single-student participation

The work is individual. If no peer is enrolled, perform the customer, process owner, analyst, control reviewer, and operator perspectives sequentially. Label each role, preserve its challenge and response, and use instructor or simulated-stakeholder feedback wherever review is required.

## Milestone 1 - Process Map and Requirements

Introduced in Week 1 and developed through Weeks 1-2.

Required evidence:
- Stakeholder contract defining customer and internal-staff outcomes, constraints, and completion evidence.
- Vendor-neutral process map with trigger, completion, roles, handoffs, data, decisions, normal flow, and one exception path.
- Requirements with user, need, behavior, value, and observable acceptance criteria.
- Decision record stating assumptions, limits, and a revision trigger.

## Milestone 2 - Vendor-Neutral Transaction Experience

Introduced in Week 3 and developed through Weeks 3-4. Carry forward Milestone 1 and record every material revision.

Required evidence:
- Transaction experience with screen states, available actions, validations, messages, authorization, reversal, and recovery.
- Data and control matrix identifying authoritative fields and preventive, detective, or corrective controls.
- Cross-functional document chain and decision dashboard with governed KPI definitions, denominators, thresholds, freshness, and drill-down.
- Tests for a normal case, boundary case, and exception case.

## Milestone 3 - Integration and Controls

Introduced in Week 5 and developed through Weeks 5-6. Carry forward Milestones 1-2 and record every material revision.

Required evidence:
- Workflow and authorization model covering approval, least privilege, segregation of duties, escalation, and controlled override.
- Vendor-neutral interface contract covering fields, states, responses, ownership, duplicate or delayed events, errors, retry, and recovery.
- Test evidence for normal, boundary, exception, access, unavailable dependency, and rollback cases.
- Monitoring and operating plan with signals, thresholds, owners, escalation, fallback, and change approval.

## Milestone 4 - Final Course Project: Integrated Process Decision System

Introduced in Week 7 and completed in Week 7.5. This is the Final Course Project. It must synthesize the prior three milestones rather than start a new scenario.

Required evidence:
- Complete integrated process design connecting customer and staff outcomes to roles, data, controls, interfaces, and decision evidence.
- Traceability matrix linking objectives, requirements, design elements, tests, results, and defects.
- Validation report with expected and actual results, corrected defects, residual risks, and a bounded release decision.
- Decision record comparing credible alternatives under shared criteria.
- Handoff runbook naming setup, ownership, operation, monitoring, escalation, fallback, and revision triggers.

## Evidence standard

Every material claim must point to an inspectable process step, record, rule, interface behavior, test result, or stakeholder observation. Another qualified person must be able to reproduce the central end-to-end trace, identify the accepted limitation, and state the exact condition under which the conclusion should change.
